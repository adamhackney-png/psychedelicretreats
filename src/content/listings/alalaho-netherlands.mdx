Alalaho runs a 5-day residential "Core Retreat" in the Netherlands (described as Amsterdam-based), typically for 10–14 participants with four facilitators — a stated ratio of around one guide per three to four participants. The format centres on a single psilocybin-truffle ceremony followed by roughly two and a half days of integration, preceded by onboarding materials, a group video call, and 24 hours of on-site preparation. Retreats are held in English, and the operator notes participants need sufficient mastery of the language.

The operator publishes a detailed screening process: a health intake form covering personal, medical and psychiatric history, followed by a one-to-one assessment call with a senior facilitator, with medical consultation for complex cases. Published exclusion criteria include personal or family history of psychosis or bipolar disorder, serious cardiovascular conditions, and conflicting medications such as SSRIs/SNRIs and antipsychotics, with a stated minimum of three months off antidepressants. Facilitators are described as seasoned facilitators and therapists who receive professional oversight and mentoring after each retreat, and the operator states it maintains detailed risk-management protocols and on-site safety planning. On legality, its materials state that truffles are legal in the Netherlands, are legally sourced and tested, and that it works within a clear legal framework, choosing venues in countries whose legal landscape supports the work.

Published pricing for the Core Retreat starts from €3,450, with truffles purchased separately at the retreat (€45 in the Netherlands), a €300 deposit, payment plans on approval, and a limited bursary program at 40% of the standard price.

This program is held in the Netherlands, where psilocybin truffles are legal to sell and possess. Psilocybin remains a prohibited substance in Australia outside the authorised clinical pathway. Australians travel at their own responsibility; this is not medical advice.
