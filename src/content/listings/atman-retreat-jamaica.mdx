Atman Retreat runs 4-day, 3-night group psilocybin retreats at a private gated villa near Montego Bay, Jamaica, founded in 2018 by Aaron Nesmith-Beck. Retreats take up to 12 participants supported by three facilitators and two additional staff, and include a group psilocybin session, guided preparation and integration activities, one-on-one facilitator check-ins, accommodation, meals and airport transfers.

Atman's materials state that retreats are held in Jamaica, where psilocybin mushrooms are legal. The application process addresses contraindications — the FAQ notes that people with personal or family histories of bipolar disorder, schizophrenia or other psychotic conditions may be at risk — and the operator states a covering physician is on call throughout psilocybin sessions. Facilitator bios published on the site describe master's-level qualifications in psychology and social work, Hakomi somatic-psychotherapy training, and MAPS-associated training backgrounds.

Published pricing is USD $2,295 (approx. A$3,280*), with a limited low-income rate of USD $395 (approx. A$565*); flights are not included.

This program is held in Jamaica, where psilocybin is not a prohibited substance. Psilocybin remains a prohibited substance in Australia outside the authorised clinical pathway. Australians travel at their own responsibility; this is not medical advice.
