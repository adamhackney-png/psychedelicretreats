This is the expanded, personal-research version of the six-item vetting checklist shown on every listing on this site. Use it as a literal question list to put to an operator before booking, by email or in a call.

## Legal & structural

1. **What is your specific legal basis for operating in this jurisdiction?** A reputable operator can answer this precisely (see our legal guide for what a good answer sounds like for each destination) — vagueness here is a signal.
2. **How long have you been operating, and under what business entity?** Operators with a documented, stable history are lower-risk than very new or frequently-renamed entities. (We flag this specifically for one operator in this directory whose corporate history includes a well-documented 2023 financial collapse — check our note on their listing.)

## Screening & medical

3. **What does your intake/screening process actually involve, and who reviews it?** Look for a named medical or psychological reviewer, not just a form.
4. **What conditions or medications would disqualify someone?** A specific contraindication list (certain psychiatric diagnoses, certain medications, pregnancy, specific cardiovascular conditions) is a stronger answer than "we assess case by case" with no detail.
5. **What's your rejection/deferral rate, if you track it?** Operators that genuinely screen out unsuitable applicants can often give you a rough figure; operators that accept nearly everyone who pays typically can't or won't.

## Facilitation

6. **Who are the facilitators, and what's their specific training?** Ask for names and credentials, not just "experienced team."
7. **What's the facilitator-to-participant ratio?** Lower ratios generally mean more individual attention during and after the experience.
8. **Is a medical professional present during the session itself, or only reachable?** These are meaningfully different levels of safety provision.

## Safety & emergency

9. **What's your specific emergency protocol, including evacuation to a hospital?** The strongest answers name a specific nearby facility and describe transport time and method, not just "we have a plan."
10. **Do you carry appropriate emergency equipment on-site** (for example, an AED, oxygen, or equivalent depending on modality)?

## Reputation & transparency

11. **Can you point me to independent reviews, not just testimonials on your own site?** Google, Trustpilot, or established third-party retreat directories are more useful than curated on-site testimonials alone.
12. **What exactly is included in the price, and what costs extra?** See our cost guide for the categories to ask about specifically.

## A note on how to read the answers

No single weak answer is necessarily disqualifying — but a pattern of vagueness across multiple questions, especially the factual ones (3, 4, 6, 9), is worth taking seriously. This list is deliberately about *specificity* of answer, not just whether an operator says yes.
