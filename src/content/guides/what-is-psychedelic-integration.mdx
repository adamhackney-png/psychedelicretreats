"Integration" comes up constantly in this space, often without being clearly defined. This guide covers what it actually means and how to find legitimate support in Australia.

## What integration means

Integration is the process of making sense of, and applying insights from, an intense psychological experience — commonly discussed in the context of psychedelic experiences, but the underlying idea (structured reflection and support after something psychologically significant) isn't unique to this context. Practitioners who describe themselves as offering psychedelic integration support are generally providing talk therapy focused specifically on this kind of processing, not administering any substance themselves.

This matters because it means integration support is a fundamentally different — and more broadly and legally accessible — category of support than a retreat or clinical psychedelic-assisted therapy. You don't need a scheduled retreat or a TGA-authorised prescription to access it.

## Why it's considered important

Intense experiences, psychedelic or otherwise, don't automatically translate into durable change on their own — the widely-held view among practitioners in this space is that what you do with an experience afterwards matters as much as the experience itself. This is general information reflecting how practitioners in this space describe their work, not a clinical claim about outcomes.
