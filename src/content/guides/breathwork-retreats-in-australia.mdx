Breathwork retreats sit in an interesting position in this directory: legally straightforward (no controlled substances involved) but not risk-free, and worth understanding on its own terms rather than as a substitute for the substance-based categories on this site.

## What breathwork actually is

"Breathwork" covers a range of specific techniques — Conscious Connected Breathwork is one commonly referenced by the Australian operators we've reviewed — generally involving a structured, often rapid breathing pattern intended to induce an altered state of consciousness without any substance. Because no substance is involved, none of the jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction legal questions covered elsewhere on this site apply — it's straightforwardly legal to practise and to offer commercially, in Australia and most other countries.

## Why people compare it to psychedelic work

Some practitioners and participants describe breathwork as producing psychologically intense states with some phenomenological similarity to other altered-state practices, which is part of why it gets discussed alongside psychedelic retreats. This is a description of how it's commonly framed, not a claim we're independently verifying about the underlying experience.

## What Australian options look like

The two operators we've reviewed for this directory both run retreats in the Byron Bay region of NSW — one offering shorter and multi-day formats combining Conscious Connected Breathwork with complementary practices like Qi Gong and yoga, the other running a longer (around 6-day) residential immersion that can count toward a breathwork practitioner certification. Neither publishes detailed screening criteria or pricing on the pages we reviewed — worth asking about directly before booking.

## Who should be cautious

Breathwork is not appropriate for everyone. Commonly cited cautions include certain cardiovascular conditions, epilepsy, pregnancy, and a history of psychosis — this is general information, not medical advice. A responsible operator will ask about these before you attend; if they don't, that's worth noting.
