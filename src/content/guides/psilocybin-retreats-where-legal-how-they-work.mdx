"Psilocybin retreat" describes a wide range of actual programs. This guide covers the structural pattern most legal operators follow, and the main ways they differ — useful context before you compare specific listings.

## The typical program shape

Across the Netherlands and Jamaica operators we've reviewed for this directory, a common pattern emerges: a multi-week program (often 7–11 weeks total) built around a much shorter in-person retreat (typically 3–6 days). The weeks before the retreat are usually virtual — preparation calls, sometimes a group cohort, and a screening/intake process. The in-person retreat itself centres on one or two guided group ceremonies, supported by facilitators, with structured time for integration built in before you leave. After you return home, several weeks of virtual integration support is common.

This "long program, short retreat" structure isn't universal — some operators run shorter, more retreat-only formats — but it's common enough among the larger, more established operators that it's worth expecting as a default and noting when a program departs from it.

## What actually varies between operators

**Screening rigor.** Some operators publish a detailed, multi-step medical and psychological screening process with an explicit contraindication list (psychosis, suicidal ideation, dissociative disorders, recent psychiatric hospitalisation are common exclusions); others describe screening only in general terms. This is one of the six items on our vetting checklist for a reason — check what's actually stated, not assumed.

**Facilitator model.** Programs differ in whether they use a purely Western clinical/therapeutic facilitation team, a traditional/Indigenous-led model, or (less commonly in the Netherlands/Jamaica context specifically, more common for ayahuasca) a blend. Check named facilitator credentials where they're published.

**Emergency protocol detail.** The strongest operators we've reviewed publish specifics — an onsite medical professional, a named nearby clinic, an evacuation plan. Weaker disclosure just says "trained staff available," which tells you less than it sounds like it does.

**Price and what's included.** Published prices in this category range widely — roughly the equivalent of AUD $6,000 to $14,000 among operators we've reviewed, before flights — and what's bundled (private vs. shared rooms, the length of the virtual program, integration calls) varies enough that comparing headline price alone is misleading. See our cost guide for a fuller breakdown.

## A note on truffles vs. mushrooms

If you're researching Netherlands operators independently, you'll see "truffles" and occasionally loose references to "mushrooms" used inconsistently across marketing copy and third-party write-ups. The legal distinction is specific and real — see our legal guide — so if an operator's own materials aren't precise about which one they use, that's worth a direct question before booking, not an assumption.
